University of Manitoba Women In Computer Science

I've spent a lot of my education in male-dominated classes. As the only woman in my IB physics class, as one of 6 in my Engineering cohort, and at the University of Manitoba, which has one of the worst ratios in Canada of womem to men enrolled in computer science. So when WICS came to speak in one of my lectures, I was immediately interested.

WICS has two goals. 1) To support and create community for women currently enrolled in computer science. 2) To encourage more young women to consider computer science related fields. I can personally atest to our success, as WICS has helped me make friends at the university, and get connected with people who have helped me get important skills and even more importantly, job interviews. It will be a few years before we see if we're successful in our second goal, but I hope that we'll start seeing WICS members who first heard of us through one of our outreach events.

Folklorama pavilions generally have four ambassadors: Adult Male, Adult Female, Youth Male, and Youth female. During the week that their pavilion is running, ambassadors play host to other ambassadors and vips. During their pavilion's off week, ambassadors tour other pavilions, representing their culture and learning about the cultures of others. Ambassadors also participate in publicity events leading up to Folklorama season.
I was lucky enough to be one of the youth ambassadors for 2013, representing the pavilion of Scotland. Being heavily involved in the gift shop normally meant that I spent the week before our pavilion run packing up boxes and planning displays, and the week after doing inventory. But that year I got to experience what other pavilions had to offer. It was a great experience, and I really tested my social skills interacting with the vips and guests.

Gift Shop Coordinator (2014 through 2017)

To attend pavilion coordinator meetings, organize and train volunteers, and act as go-between for the pavilion and the gift shop, the Pavilion of Scotland maintains the position of Gift Shop Coordinator. As someone heavily involved in Hint of Heather but not a direct owner, I was perfect for the job. I was responsible for organizing (and sometimes recruiting) volunteers, overseeing the operation of the cash register, keeping track of sales, and ensuring the functioning of the gift shop. It was during these years that I also took over a lot of responsibility planning for Folklorama, so I was also responsible for planning and setting up the displays. It was one of my first positions with real responsibility, and I'm really grateful for everything I learned.

Community Living Interlake

I got involved in Community Living Interlake when my dad started working with them on a contract basis. I had no idea that the organization even existed, despite it having been in my hometown for nearly 50 years. To this day, I haven't worked with any organizations that have more of a direct impact on peoples' lives. I have been honored to have the opportunity to help improve their website and help them display the work they're doing. Now that my dad is the Executive Director of the organization, I get to see and hear every day about how hard he and his staff are working to make life better for the individuals they support. I'm excited to see where they go, and where our new video project (explained below) can help take them.

Website Upgrades and Maintenance (Fall 2017 to present)

Check out the website

Community Living Interlake is a non-profit organization dedicated to improving the lives of individuals living with disabilities. I was asked to help improve and upgrade their website, which is hosted using Wordpress. This is an ongoing project, with changes being made at the request of the executive director.
Changes that have been made to the webiste include changes to and removal of out-of-date pages, the addition of a Facebook feed in the sidebar of the main page, and the addition of a blog-style page for the executive director to post updates.

RBC Future Launch Video Project (Fall 2019 to present)

Upon recieving a grant from RBC Future Launch, Community Living Interlake has partnered with myself and another youth leader to embark on a project entitled "Striving to live an inclusive life: Challenges and successes in one small town". The goal of the project is to create a series of videos featuring the lives of people living with disabilities, the challenges they face every day, and what we can do better. The project is being completed with the help of a team of local volunteers who hope to show off their work at a town hall at the end of the project.

Go Code Girl (Winter 2019 and 2020)

This opportunity came to me through U of M WICS, and was one of the first events I attended with them. For the 2019 event I helped the group run an activity they had also run the year before, so not much planning was required on my part. I was also a member of the lunchtime panel, talking about what it was like to be a woman studying computer science. Canada Learning Code

Generative Art with Processing

One of our WICS members was the instructor for this event hosted at the University, and when she asked for mentors to help her out I was happy to oblige. For this event for mostly elementary school kids, they were using processing to create randomized art pieces. They created simple but fun things that changed color and size each time the program started or the mouse was clicked. It was great to see young girls interested in computer science. I wish I'd been able to attend events like this one when I was a kid.

Canadian Cancer Society

Relay for Life Winnipeg (July 2016)

As part of the International Baccalaureate Program, students must complete a volunteer project. My dad has been heavily involved in the Canadian Cancer Society for most of my life, and I had always wanted to participate in the relay for life. So in spring of 2015, I decided to try and pull together a team.

It didn't work. I started too late, and I couldn't get enough people involved quickly enough.

So at the beginning of the next school year, I took what I had learned the year before and started recruiting. I managed to pull together a small team and complete a few fundraising events that included a cotton candy sale, and an addition to a school pep rally that let students pay to get their favorite (or least favorite) teacher pied in the face. We made it to the relay and set up a booth selling some crafts and snacks to other participants, and to this day it's one of my proudest accomplishments. I took initiative to build my own team from nothing, and then again from a failure, and I learned so much on the way.
